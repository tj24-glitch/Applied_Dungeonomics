Applied Dungeonomics

I used to believe Dungeon Mastering was about storytelling, collaboration, and carefully weaving player backstories into a cohesive narrative.

Then I ran multiple campaigns where the rogue committed arson “for efficiency,” the bard attempted diplomacy using vibes, eye contact, and lies, and the barbarian said “I throw the halfling. He consented. Emotionally.”

Applied Dungeonomics is what happens when a Dungeon Master stops asking why the party did something and starts asking how often it keeps happening. This is a statistically grounded, emotionally compromised attempt to quantify player behavior, narrative instability, and the exact moment a campaign leaves the rails and goes into the woods — because it was somehow convinced it was a tree.

These charts are not judgments. They are documentation for the inevitable tribunal.

How to Use This (Please Read Before Drawing Conclusions)

This visualization is designed to be explored one index at a time. Each index represents a specific, extremely common D&D scenario that has historically ended badly for someone.

  • Select an index using the buttons at the top.
  • The chart shows how characters distribute across outcome tiers for that scenario.
  • Background colors are interpretation bands — not all scores mean the same thing.
  • Click a band (or its legend entry) to see which race + class combinations most commonly appear in that range.
  • If a race or class is missing or unknown, hover to view the associated field report explaining why the data could not be recovered.

A final note: rare outcomes are rare on purpose. If you encounter a character in the extreme upper ranges, please remain calm and notify your Dungeon Master.

Dedicated to my brother, our long‑suffering DM, who survived campaign prep, player chaos, and irreversible psychic damage from the words “can I do something weird?”
